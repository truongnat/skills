# Competitor and positioning

## Competitor map

- Classify: direct, adjacent, substitute.
- Segment by target customer, pricing band, distribution model.
- Compare current traction signals (customers, case studies, integrations, hiring signals).

## Positioning framework

- Problem statement and buyer role.
- Category anchor and differentiation wedge.
- Proof points: measurable outcomes, defensibility, switching-cost dynamics.

## Comparison table

- Feature parity is not enough; include onboarding friction, support model, ecosystem fit, and compliance posture.
- Separate "table stakes" from true differentiators.

## Risks

- Over-indexing feature lists without evaluating GTM execution.
- Using outdated pricing pages; verify latest public sources.

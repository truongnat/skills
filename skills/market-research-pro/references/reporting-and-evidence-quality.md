# Reporting and evidence quality

## Source quality rubric

- Prefer primary and official sources.
- Record publication date, author/publisher, and methodology transparency.
- Flag estimates from opaque or marketing-only sources.

## Report structure

1. Decision context and scope.
2. Findings summary.
3. Evidence and assumptions.
4. Recommendation and confidence.
5. Risks and follow-up data to collect.

## Confidence guidance

- Use high/medium/low per major conclusion.
- Lower confidence when assumptions dominate or sources conflict.

## Contradiction handling

- Show conflicting evidence side by side.
- Explain why one source is weighted higher.
